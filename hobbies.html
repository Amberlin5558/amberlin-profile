Life Beyond the Screen

I believe a rich life fuels a sharp mind. My hobbies are where I cultivate discipline, explore my creativity, and connect with the world. They are not just pastimes, but essential practices that shape my perspective as a product person—fostering empathy, resilience, and a deep-seated curiosity.

Fitness & Pilates

Pilates and regular fitness are my anchors. They teach me discipline, focus, and the power of consistent effort. This practice in physical precision and control directly translates to a more structured and resilient approach to tackling complex product challenges.

Reading & Learning

From "Yu Jun's Product Methodology" to "The Power of Now," books and podcasts like "Liang Ning's Product Thinking" are my tools for continuous growth. They keep my mind sharp and ensure I'm always learning from the best in the field.

Travel & Exploration

Exploring new places, like the serene temples of Kyoto or Take the Swiss train and climb the mountain to see the beautiful Alps, broadens my perspective. It's the ultimate lesson in user research—observing diverse cultures and understanding different ways of life.

Cooking as Creation

Crafting a meal is a creative process, much like developing a product. It's about balancing ingredients (features), presentation (UI), and taste (user experience) to create something delightful and satisfying.

Puzzles & Problem-Solving

Piecing together a puzzle requires patience and a methodical approach. It's a satisfying exercise in seeing how individual components connect to form a cohesive whole—a perfect metaphor for systems thinking.

Life with a Pet

My corgi Simba brings daily joy and a valuable lesson in empathy and responsibility. Understanding the needs of another being is the foundation of user-centric design.
